## Journal Publications

### 1. A human-robot collaboration model empowered by object detection-driven AR assistance and operator behavior prediction

**Year:** 2026  

**Research areas:**
- Human-Robot Collaboration
- Hidden Semi-Markov Models
- Augmented Reality
- Collaborative Assembly Systems

**Summary:**  
This study proposes a bidirectional human-robot collaboration model that combines AR assistance for operators and HSMM prediction for cobots. Testing shows this approach boosts assembly efficiency by 13% and cuts mental workload by 9.6% in flexible manufacturing settings.

### 3. Modeling of human–robot collaboration for flexible assembly—a hidden semi-Markov-based simulation approach

**Year:** 2023  

**Research areas:**
- Human-Robot Collaboration
- Hidden Semi-Markov Models
- Simulation modeling
- Collaborative Assembly Systems
- Human Factors Engineering


**Summary:**  
This study introduces a simulation-based model for Human–Robot Collaboration (HRC) in flexible assembly to avoid the risks and costs of real-world testing. Using a hidden semi-Markov model, the system predicts an operator's risk perception based on their assembly speed, allowing the robot to intelligently adjust its movements. Tested on graphics card assembly, the approach successfully reduced cycle time and robot idle time while ensuring safety.
